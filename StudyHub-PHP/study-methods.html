Spaced Repetition

What it is:

Reviewing information at increasing intervals to combat the forgetting curve.

How to use it:

  • Use apps like Anki or Quizlet
  • Review material just before you're about to forget it
  • Increase intervals after each successful recall
Pro Tip: Start with 1-2-4-7 day intervals for new material.

Active Recall

What it is:

Retrieving information from memory rather than passive re-reading.

How to use it:

  • Close your book and recite what you remember
  • Use practice tests even before you feel ready
  • Create question flashcards instead of fact cards
Pro Tip: The harder the recall feels, the stronger the learning.

Blurting

What it is:

A self-check method where you write down everything you know from memory after studying a topic.

How to use it:

  • Read or watch your study material once
  • Close everything and write what you remember
  • Compare and fill in missing points
Pro Tip: Blurting works best before revision ‚Äî it shows what you *actually* remember.

Feynman Technique

What it is:

Learning by teaching ‚Äî explaining a concept in simple terms reveals what you truly understand.

How to use it:

  • Pick a concept and explain it like you're teaching someone with no background
  • Identify confusing parts and review them
  • Repeat until you can explain clearly and confidently
Pro Tip: Try writing explanations in a notebook as if you're tutoring your past self.

Mind Mapping

What it is:

A visual method to organize ideas by connecting them like branches of a tree.

How to use it:

  • Start with a central idea in the middle
  • Add key subtopics as branches
  • Use keywords, colors, and images to boost memory
Pro Tip: Use mind maps for essay planning, summaries, or linking complex topics.

SQ3R Method

What it is:

A 5-step method for reading comprehension: Survey, Question, Read, Recite, Review.

How to use it:

  • Survey: Skim titles and summaries first
  • Question: Turn headings into questions
  • Read: Actively read for answers
  • Recite: Say answers out loud
  • Review: Go over key points again
Pro Tip: Use SQ3R when reading textbooks to stay engaged and focused.
